Interactive Experience Research Project

For this website review, I have chose the Luni website based on my own experience browsing it and the feelings it created while I interacted with its design, movement, and playful digital atmosphere. Overall, I was a fan of the interactive elements and the unique 3D design, which blends the pastel colours with a Vaporwave aesthetic.

Luni.app

1] What was the first thing I paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

  • Some of the things I paid attention when browsing the site was the vibrant pastel colours used and playful animations of some of the objects and interactable gimmicks on certain pages.

    • 2] List of common discrete actions done while exploring the website:

  • The most common actions I have done while exploring the website were clicking on interactable gimmicks and hovering around the 3D space of the main page. I also played some of the minigames on the Omada section of the website, trying to score as many points as possible.

    • 3] Parts and experiences that I spent most time engaging with:

  • I spent most of my time playing and interacting with the small minigames, because they were both fun and visually engaging. What made them interesting was the social element, such as the scoreboards and points system, showing scores from other visitors of the website. This created a sort of community among people of similar interests, connecting them with others who share the same passions.

    • 4] What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?

  • The most common action during my two-minute interaction with the website was exploring the 3D space on each page, I kept rotating, hovering and experimentingaround different parts of the space and admiring the 3D web design. I was curious and intrigued with how the website would respond with its 3D design, making me want to keep testing its interactive features.

    • 5] My impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

  • My impression of the intended primary goal of the website is that it aims to present Luni as a creative, innovative and playful brand by creating an engaging and immersive environment that showcases the company's innovative approach to digital media. The experience combines playful elements like the laptop, punching bag, frying pan and the minigames with cutting-edge 3D design to provide a unique, quirky, playful and memorable user journey. This makes the experience feel more like a digital exploration of a 3D world than a typical corporate website.

    • 6] How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

  • This interactive experience communicate the idea that this company is outgoing, innovative, and creative. A company that is trying to present itself as fun and unique with its cheerful visual style, interactive design choices, and strong sense of originality. It also presents a distinct identity that is forward-thinking and experimental with their fresh and unconventional approach to digital media, setting it apart in the digital media landscape.

    • 7] My impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

  • In my opinion, the website is designed to be explored over multiple visits rather than all at once. It is best in short sessions of 15 minutes each and spread across 3 different visits. With the many animations, various ways to navigate the 3D space and small minigames of the webpage, it will keep the visitor intrigued and engaged for a while to play around and gradually discover interactive gimmicks and minigames. This makes the website feel like something you will want to come back to, rather than a one-time

    • 8] How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

  • Over time, interacting with the website encouraged me to slow down and appreciate the design choices instead of rushing through the content. The company is using a unique and innovative approach to digital media, which encourages exploration, experimentation and repeated discovery, the experience feel more like a journey than a typical website where more time spent on the website, more secrets start to unfold gradually.

    • 9] What other forms of media (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

  • The experience referenced and draws heavy inspirations from Vaporwave art, which creates a nostalgic yet futuristic mood through pastel colors, retro-like style and 80s inspired aesthetics, taking inspiration from the artstyle of games like Paradise Killer and Verlet Swing which shares a similarly distinct and expressive visual identity. It also references old school arcade games through the use of scoreboard, competition and minigame mechanics, making the website feel playful and game-like.

    • 10] What does this reference/s communicate about how I should act when engaging with the research experience?

  • These references encouraged me interact more playfully and experiment with the website. Instead of treating it as a traditional corporate website, the various interactable gimmicks made me click, test the 3D features in each webpage, and see what happens. The game elements made me feel I should explore the website, rather than browsing.

    • 11] What does this reference/s communicate about how I should feel when engaging with the research experience?

  • Engaging with the website encouraged me to feel more relaxed, curious, and entertained, rather than neutral or informational. The pastel colour palette of the website gives a lighthearted mood that felt different from the serious tone of most business websites. It made me feel that that I was invited to explore their creative space while also gether information and learning more about the company

    • 12] What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

  • One of the most frustrating part of the experience was the horizontal scrolling section in the Studio page, especially where the team members and roles are displayed. The contents moves horizontally while scrolling vertically, making it difficult to read everything clearly and keep track of the names and job titles. This made the section feel less accessible and slightly awkward to navigate.

    • 13] What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

  • The most satisfying part of the interaction was the sense of discovery and exploration within the 3D space. The way the environment responded to my actions and the visual feedback provided a sense of accomplishment and engagement that made the experience enjoyable. The transitions from the various pages were satisfying. The smooth and seamless transition when I select a certain category of the page to the destination was crisp and effective. It looks as if I was teleported to another level of a game, which portrays the company's fun and creative character.
